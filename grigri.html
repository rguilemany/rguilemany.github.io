Grigri Pixel Barcelona 2017

Collaborative furniture workshop | Product design
2017


Grigri is the name given to the amulets that are used in West Africa to protect themselves from bad luck.

Grigri Pixel is a collective learning experiment in search of urban grigris capable of taking care of the spaces we share and resisting the impoverishment and privatization of our cities.

Through processes of mediation, open design, artistic practices and digital manufacturing, Grigri Pixel operate as a collaborative research-active laboratory where the exchange of knowledge and experiences helps us create grigris or protective objects (in the form of furniture) for the reactivation of urban ecosystems.

Here you can get a better approach form philosopher Marina Garcés.

Grigri Pixel Barcelona 2017 build the Grigri of Pasaje Trullàs (Poblenou, Barcelona). It has been developed in response to a series of demands, needs and opportunities identified through a participatory diagnosis with the neighbors and of the area.

It consists of four elements that work in a modular way, both separately and together. They are mobile and interactive elements that communicate with each other through their lighting, powered by solar panels.

This project has been carried out with the collaboration of:

Hangar.org | BAU (design college of Barcelona) | ENORME studio | Medialab Prado | CEI UPM (center for industrial electronics) | La Taula Eix Pere IV | GREDITS (group of research in design and social transformation) | Cooperacion Española | And all the people that volunteered for the project!
